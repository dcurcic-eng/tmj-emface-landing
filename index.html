TMJ Disorder Treatment

Non-Invasive Relief for TMJ & Jaw Tension

Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder affects millions of people, causing chronic jaw pain, headaches, clicking, and difficulty chewing. Emface's HIFES technology targets the masseter, temporalis, and pterygoid muscles — the key muscle groups involved in TMJ dysfunction. By delivering precise electrical stimulation, Emface helps relax hypertonic jaw muscles, reduce clenching patterns, and restore balanced muscle function. Combined with radiofrequency energy that promotes deep tissue circulation and healing, the treatment addresses both muscular and inflammatory components of TMJ disorder — all without medication, injections, or surgical intervention.

Symptoms We Treat

Do You Suffer From These TMJ Symptoms?

Emface therapy at Eternal Wellness is designed for patients experiencing a range of TMJ-related symptoms, including chronic jaw pain or soreness, teeth grinding (bruxism), tension headaches, earaches and tinnitus, jaw locking or limited range of motion, and facial muscle fatigue. Traditional treatments often rely on night guards, pain medication, or invasive procedures. Emface offers a fundamentally different approach — rehabilitating the jaw musculature directly through targeted neuromuscular stimulation while promoting tissue recovery with therapeutic heat.

Submentum Treatment

Medical-Grade Submentum & Lower Face Therapy

The submentum region — the area beneath the chin and along the jawline — is medically significant beyond appearance. Weakness in the platysma, digastric, and suprahyoid muscle groups can contribute to functional issues including airway narrowing, swallowing difficulties, and postural imbalances. Emface Submentum uses HIFES to strengthen and rehabilitate these muscles, while radiofrequency energy promotes tissue integrity and circulation. This treatment is particularly beneficial for patients recovering from muscle atrophy, those with age-related submental laxity, or individuals seeking a non-surgical approach to improving lower face and neck muscle tone.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Emface uses HIFES (High-Intensity Focused Electrical Stimulation) to target the masseter, temporalis, and pterygoid muscles — the primary muscle groups involved in TMJ dysfunction. The technology relaxes overworked, hypertonic jaw muscles while restoring balanced neuromuscular function. Simultaneously, radiofrequency energy promotes circulation and tissue healing in the joint area, addressing both muscular and inflammatory components of TMJ disorder.

Most patients describe the sensation as a warm, tingling feeling combined with gentle muscle contractions. The treatment is comfortable and requires no anesthesia. Many TMJ patients report feeling immediate relief in jaw tension during and after the 20-minute session.

A typical therapeutic protocol consists of 4 sessions spaced 5–10 days apart. Many patients notice improvement after the first treatment, with progressive relief building over the full course. Maintenance sessions every 6–12 months can help sustain long-term results depending on the severity of your condition.

Emface Submentum specifically targets the muscles and tissue beneath the chin and along the jawline — including the platysma, digastric, and suprahyoid muscles. Using HIFES to strengthen weakened muscles and RF energy to improve tissue integrity, it addresses submental laxity, muscle atrophy, and related functional concerns without surgery.

Emface can serve as a standalone treatment or as a complement to your existing TMJ management plan (e.g., night guards, physical therapy). During your consultation, our clinical team will assess your condition and recommend the best approach for integrating Emface into your care.

Emface is not recommended for individuals who are pregnant, have electronic implants (such as pacemakers or defibrillators), metal implants in the treatment area, or active infections in the jaw or facial region. A consultation with our team will determine your eligibility based on your specific medical history.
