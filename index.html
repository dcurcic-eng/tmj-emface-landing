TMD Treatment

Non-invasive relief for TMD & jaw tension

Temporomandibular disorder (TMD) affects millions of people, causing chronic jaw pain, headaches, clicking, and difficulty chewing. Emface's HIFES technology targets the masseter, temporalis, and pterygoid muscles, the key muscle groups involved in TMJ dysfunction. By delivering precise electrical stimulation, Emface helps relax hypertonic jaw muscles, reduce clenching patterns, and restore balanced muscle function. Combined with radiofrequency energy that promotes deep tissue circulation and healing, the treatment addresses both muscular and inflammatory components of TMD, all without medication, injections, or surgical intervention.

Symptoms We Treat

Do you suffer from these TMD symptoms?

Emface therapy at Eternal Wellness is designed for patients experiencing a range of TMD-related symptoms, including chronic jaw pain or soreness, teeth grinding (bruxism), tension headaches, earaches and tinnitus, jaw locking or limited range of motion, and facial muscle fatigue. Traditional treatments often rely on night guards, pain medication, or invasive procedures. Emface offers a fundamentally different approach: rehabilitating the jaw musculature directly through targeted neuromuscular stimulation while promoting tissue recovery with therapeutic heat.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Emface uses HIFES (High-Intensity Focused Electrical Stimulation) to target the masseter, temporalis, and pterygoid muscles, the primary muscle groups involved in TMJ dysfunction. The technology relaxes overworked, hypertonic jaw muscles while restoring balanced neuromuscular function. Simultaneously, radiofrequency energy promotes circulation and tissue healing in the joint area, addressing both muscular and inflammatory components of TMD.

Most patients describe the sensation as a warm, tingling feeling combined with gentle muscle contractions. The treatment is comfortable and requires no anesthesia. Many TMJ patients report feeling immediate relief in jaw tension during and after the 20-minute session.

A typical therapeutic protocol consists of 4 sessions spaced 5 to 10 days apart. Many patients notice improvement after the first treatment, with progressive relief building over the full course. Maintenance sessions every 6 to 12 months can help sustain long-term results depending on the severity of your condition.

Emface can serve as a standalone treatment or as a complement to your existing TMD management plan (e.g., night guards, physical therapy). During your consultation, our clinical team will assess your condition and recommend the best approach for integrating Emface into your care.

Emface is not recommended for individuals who are pregnant, have electronic implants (such as pacemakers or defibrillators), metal implants in the treatment area, or active infections in the jaw or facial region. A consultation with our team will determine your eligibility based on your specific medical history.
